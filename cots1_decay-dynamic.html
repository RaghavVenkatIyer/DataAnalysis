Apogee & perigee altitude

Drag concentrates at perigee where density is highest. Energy bleed first cuts apogee; perigee barely moves until the orbit is much more circular.

Semi-major axis a

a sets the orbit's total mechanical energy via ε = -μ/(2a). A monotonic drop in a is direct evidence of energy loss.

Eccentricity e

Apogee falls faster than perigee, so the orbit is becoming less elliptical. Watch e creep down as the orbit circularizes.

Orbital period

A shorter period means a smaller, lower-energy orbit. By Kepler's third law (T² ∝ a³), the period change tracks Δa exactly.

Specific orbital energy ε

Computed at every epoch as ε = -μ/(2a). The slope is the instantaneous power dissipated by drag, per unit mass.

BSTAR drag term

SGP4's empirical drag coefficient, a proxy for the instantaneous (ρ · CD · A / 2m) the propagator infers. Spikes correlate with elevated atmospheric density (geomagnetic activity, solar EUV).

How the atmospheric loss was computed

All values come from the loaded OMM/TLE elements. The orbit is two-body Keplerian; every difference between epochs is non-conservative force — overwhelmingly atmospheric drag at low-Earth-orbit altitudes, with negligible solar pressure for a body this dense.
